Debug logs are a type of log files that contain detailed information about a system's operations, particularly when it comes to identifying and resolving issues. Because of their high level of detail, debug logs can be resource-intensive to generate and store.

In summary, debug logs are highly detailed log files used for diagnosing and fixing specific issues within a system. They can be resource-intensive to generate and store, and their primary purpose is to aid in troubleshooting rather than long-term analysis or monitoring.
